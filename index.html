🎥 Lecture: Newton's Laws of Motion (Demo)
📜 Video Transcript (AI ready)
Speaker: Newton's first law states that an object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and in the same direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced force. This is the law of inertia. Second law: Force equals mass times acceleration (F = ma). Third law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. These principles form the foundation of classical mechanics. In today's lecture, we'll explore real-world applications - from rockets to car safety. Remember, understanding inertia helps us predict motion in everyday life.
